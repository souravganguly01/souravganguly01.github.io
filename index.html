About

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the Department of Earth and Planetary Sciences at Weizmann Institute of Science, Israel. My research centers on fluidâ€“rock interactions across diverse spatial and temporal scales, and how these processes shape Earth systems. My recent works have focused on developing novel metal isotope tools, constraining low-temperature water-rock interactions, weathering of silicates and a variety of aqueous geochemical processes in rivers and groundwater aquifers.

I integrate real-world field observations with geochemical and isotopic analyses of water, sediment, and rock samples, alongside geochemical modeling, to advance our understanding of these fundamental Earth processes.
